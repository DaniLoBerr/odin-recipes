Asturian Cachopo

Description

A Cachopo is a traditional dish of Asturian cuisine which is made from two large fillets of beef between which it is stuffed with Iberian ham and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 2 fillets of IGP Asturian Beef.
  • 4 thin slices of Asturian Cheese.
  • 4 thin slices of Iberian Ham.
  • 100 gr white flour.
  • 2 eggs.
  • 200g breadcrumbs.
  • Salt.
  • Pepper.
  • Olive Oil.

Steps

  1. Pound the beef fillets to flatten and tenderise them.
  2. Place two slices of cheese on one fillet, two slices of Serrano ham on top of the cheese and repeat the process with two more slices of cheese and ham on top.
  3. Cover with the other fillet, seal the edges well and put in the freezer for 10 minutes.
  4. Season the cachopo with salt, pepper and flour.
  5. Dip it in the beaten egg.
  6. Bread the cachopo, taking care not to leave any holes.
  7. Leave the breaded cachopo to rest for half an hour.
  8. Fry in plenty of hot oil and leave to rest on absorbent paper.
